Italy Trip — June 2024

Flew into Rome on June 1st and stayed for 5 days in Trastevere. Chose Trastevere specifically because it felt more like a real neighborhood than the touristy center — cobblestone streets, locals sitting outside in the evenings, laundry hanging between buildings. Walked everywhere. Rome is a city that rewards getting lost.

First full day was the obvious stuff — Colosseum, Roman Forum, Palatine Hill. Bought a combined ticket and spent the whole morning there. The Forum was more interesting than expected, easy to spend two hours just wandering through it. Threw a coin in the Trevi Fountain at 7am before the crowds arrived, completely different experience than the photos suggest when it's not wall to wall tourists.

Vatican on day three. Booked an early entry ticket which was absolutely worth it — inside by 8am, first hour was almost peaceful. The Sistine Chapel still managed to be packed but the galleries leading up to it were incredible and mostly quiet. St. Peter's Basilica is genuinely one of the most impressive buildings I've ever stood in. Climbed to the top of the dome for views over the whole city.

Food in Rome was simple and perfect. Ate cacio e pepe three times. Found a small trattoria near the apartment that had no menu, the owner just told you what was good that day. Had suppli from a street counter for lunch twice. Gelato from a place on the corner of our street every single night — pistachio and dark chocolate.

Day 6 took a morning train to Florence and stayed for 3 nights. Florence is smaller and more walkable than Rome. The Uffizi Gallery was the main event — booked tickets in advance, spent four hours inside, could have spent more. Seeing Botticelli's Birth of Venus in person after only knowing it from art history books was a strange and moving experience. Climbed to the top of the Duomo which required booking a timed slot — the views over Florence's terracotta rooftops were worth every step.

Ate bistecca alla fiorentina at a restaurant near the Mercato Centrale that a local recommended. Massive, perfectly cooked, shared between two people and still barely finished it. The Mercato Centrale itself was great for lunch — multiple vendors, fresh pasta, lampredotto sandwiches which are a Florence specialty and not for the faint hearted.

Last stop was three nights in the Cinque Terre, staying in Vernazza which is the prettiest of the five villages. Completely different pace from Rome and Florence — tiny, colorful, perched on cliffs above the sea. Hiked the trail between Vernazza and Monterosso in the morning which was steep and spectacular.
